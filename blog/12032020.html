Coronavirus Shutdown? Call it a Tech Debt sprint!

Last week, I was on my first business trip to India. I have many Indian friends and I was very excited to look around and try some great food whilst working with some local people. Around 12 hours after landing I had a phone call asking me to return home immediately.
Despite the paranoia, the British seem to be keeping a stiff upper lip, whilst secretly clearing stores of their pasta supplies.
It looks like many of us are starting a period of working from home. Meetings will be remote and the cabin fever will be intense.
Here is my approach:

  • Make the most of this time. It is rare that we get the chance to reflect. Use this time to improve your wellbeing, documentation or finally tackle some projects that have been in the back of your mind!
  • Exercise every day. Viruses seem to do worse outside and it will reduce the cabin fever. Your immune system will thank you too.
  • Organize a Virtual Coffee Meeting with colleagues. No agenda, just catch up. I recently arranged these after reading about GitLabs approach. This will help to deal the isolation.
  • Don't worry about the virus. Buddha told us that life is suffering. Stoics told us not to worry about what we can't control. Enjoy the ride!
